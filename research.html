Research

My research focuses on fluid mechanics, particularly the behavior of multiphase systems, interfacial flows, and capillary-scale phenomena. I use a combination of high-speed imaging, theoretical modeling, and experimental techniques to understand the physics behind the motion, breakup, and interaction of liquid drops and beads with their environment.

Current Topics

  • Bead-on-fiber dynamics and pattern formation
  • Drop rebound and contact line instabilities
  • Capillary-driven flow along wetting and non-wetting surfaces
  • Particle-laden interface dynamics and stability

Past Work

My earlier projects have included studies on droplet impact on soft surfaces, water entry of particles, and interfacial phenomena in confined geometries. For more detailed descriptions and results, please refer to the Publications page.
