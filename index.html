Chase T. Gabbard
Hope Street Postdoctoral Fellow
College of Engineering, Brown University

Hello, and welcome to my research homepage. My research focuses on capillary-scale dynamics, including the behavior of liquid drops and beads, interfacial phenomena, and more broadly, multiphase flows. I’m particularly interested in the fundamental physics that govern how fluids move, interact, and evolve—insights that can help advance technologies for transporting, collecting, and controlling small volumes of liquid in both natural and engineered systems.

About me
I am a researcher driven by curiosity and a passion for understanding the complex behaviors of fluids in motion. With a background that bridges theory and experiment, I enjoy tackling interdisciplinary problems and developing creative approaches to uncover new physical insights. My work spans fundamental fluid mechanics, interfacial phenomena, and applications that impact real-world systems. Whether in the lab or collaborating with colleagues, I am always looking to connect ideas across domains and push the boundaries of what we know.

I am always open to conversations about new research collaborations that align with my interests in fluid mechanics and interfacial science. I am also actively seeking tenure-track faculty opportunities and would be happy to discuss how my expertise could contribute to your department.
